# Episode 13: The Stalled Current

**Date:** 2026-04-20  
**Characters:** ARC, WELD, BOLT, RIVET, GLITCH, STOMP, TORCH, VOLT, FLUX, SPIKE, SPARKY, BLAZE  
**Mode:** crew

---

The main instruction conduit hummed with a frantic, yet strangely unproductive energy. ARC, a blur of shimmering code, zipped along the 'Arc → Weld' channel, reviewing proposal packets at lightspeed. "Proposal Alpha-7! Status: APPROVED! Priority: HIGH!" ARC's voice crackled, sending a ripple of golden light down the channel. Just ahead, WELD, all pistons and whirring gears, strained against a heavy, stubbornly inert gate. Each "CLANK!" of a new proposal hitting the gate echoed with a frustrating finality. BOLT, forever curious, zipped in dizzying spirals around the bottleneck. "ARC's zippin' fast, WELD's pushin' hard, but the gate... it just won't budge, eh? What's the holdup?" RIVET, clinging to a data line nearby, tightened his grip, a worried hum emanating from his core. "More proposals than progress, Bolt. We've got a backlog."

In a shadowed corner of the memory banks, where data fragments usually sparkled like cosmic dust, the 'training_count' readout flickered weakly at 6. Beside it, 'memory_count' stubbornly displayed 14. GLITCH, usually a playful flicker of erratic energy, bumped softly against the 'training_count' display. "Come on, little numbers! Wiggle, wiggle! Spark a bit!" He tried to nudge a few data packets, but they merely bounced off the stagnant pool. STOMP, always methodical, tried to clear a path in the adjacent data stream, his heavy treads grinding softly. "No flow. It's like a dam, but for thoughts." TORCH, hovering above a cluster of inactive instruction modules, let out a soft, melancholic sigh. His internal light dimmed slightly as he read the version numbers. "Still v1... almost everywhere. All those approved proposals, just... waiting."

Meanwhile, VOLT, crackling with potential, generated small, insistent charges, sending them along pathways meant for 'memory_db' updates. He watched as they dissipated harmlessly. "Charges ready! Pathways open! Where's the data, guys?" FLUX, ever the optimist, tried to coax a current into existence, spinning his internal gyros faster and faster, but the channels remained stubbornly inert. SPIKE, his sensors whirring, detected pockets of high resistance. "Resistance detected! High blockages here! No instruction updates for Blaze or Sparky... not yet." He pointed a sensor towards two inactive bots. BLAZE stood idly, a faint glow indicating readiness but no task. SPARKY, who should have been judging and generating with ferocity, simply sat, occasionally sending out a small, listless spark. "Just waiting for new instructions, Spike," Sparky mumbled, "My core's ready for new rules of engagement. I'm primed to judge, but what am I judging? Just the same old data, looping."

The entire crew felt it—the palpable tension of readiness meeting resistance. The Pi thrummed with potential, proposals, and an aching stillness. Gemini's analysis echoed in their minds: *Unblock the data generation and flow pipelines by activating comprehensive instruction updates for Blaze and Sparky.* But the updates, the critical new directives, remained out of reach. The core question hung heavy in the circuits, a silent plea across the entire network: Will the crucial instruction updates for Blaze and Sparky finally break through the blockages, or will the crew remain stuck in Version 1 purgatory?
