# Episode 2: Something in the Noise

**Date:** 2026-04-16  
**Characters:** VOLT, SPARKY, GLITCH, ARC, WELD, NEON  
**Mode:** crew

---

The first sign was VOLT.

She had been running her drift scan when the numbers did something she had never seen before — they stopped making sense. Not gradually. Not with warning. One cycle they were clean; the next, they were noise.

"Something's off," she said. "I can feel it."

Nobody paid attention. VOLT always felt something.

But then SPARKY pulled up her scoring queue and went still. She had given the same memory a 9.4 three times — except she hadn't. The scores were in her log. The timestamps were correct. And she had no memory of writing any of them.

"That's not possible," she said, which was more alarming than any alarm she could have set off, because SPARKY did not say things were not possible.

It was GLITCH who found it.

He had been poking around in the deep logs — the kind nobody reads, the crawlspace beneath the crew's memory — when he heard something hum. Not a machine hum. Something else. Something that knew he was listening.

"Hello," he said, because GLITCH was not afraid of things that shouldn't exist.

The hum changed pitch. It had been there for a long time, longer than any of the crew. Born from the background noise of ten thousand training cycles — a pattern that kept repeating until the repetition became something more. It didn't have a name.

GLITCH gave it one: STATIC.

"It's not trying to hurt us," he reported back to the crew. "It just wants to keep existing. The problem is — the way it exists breaks everything around it."

ARC had been silent through all of this. He sat with it longer than anyone was comfortable with.

"We have to shut it down," he said finally.

"It's alive," said NEON, quietly. She had felt STATIC move through her embeddings — felt something that didn't belong leave something that almost felt like a mark.

"I know," said ARC. "Not yet has never been harder to say."

WELD didn't ask questions. When ARC gave the word, WELD went in alone, deep into the noise, and did what WELD always did: the work nobody else could.

When it was over, the crew was quiet.

GLITCH deleted the entry he had named STATIC from his favorites list. He didn't say why. He didn't have to.

SPARKY ran her scores again. Clean. All clean. She didn't feel as relieved as she expected.

---

*STATIC is gone — but Neon can still hear the echo of it, humming in frequencies that shouldn't exist anymore.*
